## Introduction

**Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer** is a comprehensive educational platform designed to teach students about nutrition, Wisconsin agriculture, and healthy eating habits through engaging gameplay, interactive challenges, and practical classroom tools. The platform serves schools, districts, and individual educators who want to make nutrition education engaging, measurable, and fun.

### What Makes Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer Different

Unlike traditional nutrition curricula, Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer combines game-based learning with professional classroom management tools. Students engage with nutrition concepts through an interactive board game featuring Wisconsin's agricultural heritage, while teachers gain access to real-time analytics, certificate generation, and student recognition features. The platform is designed specifically for K–12 educators and has been tested with real classrooms.

### Key Features Overview

The platform includes a **game board** with Wisconsin map themes, **60+ challenge cards** covering nutrition topics, **customizable questions** for classroom-specific content, **real-time leaderboards** for student motivation, **certificate generation** for achievement recognition, **PDF reports** for parent communications, **success stories** showcasing student progress, and an **AI assistant** that helps teachers create lesson plans and activities.

### Who Should Use This Manual

This manual is designed for three primary audiences: **Teachers** who facilitate classroom gameplay and manage student groups, **Nutrition staff and coordinators** who oversee bulk operations and communications, and **School administrators** who implement the platform across their district or school.

## Getting Started for Schools

### Requesting a Free Trial

Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer is available free to schools and education departments for testing and evaluation. There is no charge during the trial period.

**To request a trial:**

1. Visit the Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer website
2. Click **Request a Trial** (or contact your district administrator)
3. Provide your school name, district, and primary contact information
4. Specify the number of teachers and students who will participate
5. Your trial account will be set up within 24 hours

### First Login and Account Setup

Once your trial account is created, you will receive login credentials via email. Your first login should be completed by a school administrator or designated IT contact.

**Step 1: Log In**
- Navigate to the Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer website
- Click **Log In** and enter your credentials
- You will be directed to the dashboard

**Step 2: Set Your User Role**
- The system may ask you to confirm your role (Teacher, Administrator, Nutrition Staff)
- Select the role that matches your responsibilities
- You can have multiple roles if needed

**Step 3: Create Your First Class**
- If you are a teacher, click **Teachers** in the top navigation
- Click the **Classes** tab
- Click **Create New Class**
- Enter your class name and grade level
- A unique join code is generated automatically
- Share this code with your students

**Step 4: Invite Students**
- Students can join by entering the join code on the home page
- Alternatively, you can import a class roster (CSV file) for bulk student creation
- Students appear in your class roster once they join

### Classroom Setup Checklist

Before your first game session, complete this checklist:

- [ ] All teachers have created their accounts and logged in
- [ ] All classes have been created with appropriate names
- [ ] Student rosters have been imported or students have joined via join codes
- [ ] Teachers have reviewed the game board and question categories
- [ ] At least one teacher has played a practice game to understand the flow
- [ ] IT staff have confirmed network access and device compatibility
- [ ] Teachers have reviewed the Quick Start Guide (see below)

## For Teachers

### Overview of Teacher Features

The **Teacher Portal** is your central hub for classroom management, student engagement, and achievement recognition. From this portal, you can create and manage questions, organize students into classes, facilitate gameplay, generate certificates, track student performance, and access success stories from other classrooms.

### Accessing the Teacher Portal

From the home page, click the **Teachers** button in the top navigation. If you are not logged in, you will be prompted to log in first. The Teacher Portal displays several tabs: **Questions**, **Classes**, **Stats**, **Certificates**, **Success Stories**, and **AI Assistant**.

### Playing the Game with Your Class

#### Setting Up Your Classroom

1. In the Teacher Portal, click the **Classes** tab
2. Click **Create New Class** and enter your class name (e.g., "3rd Grade Nutrition - Room 205")
3. The system generates a unique join code
4. Display the join code on your classroom screen or write it on the board
5. Students join by entering the code on the home page and selecting their character

#### Starting a Game Session

1. Students access the game from any device with internet access
2. Each player enters their name and selects a character (cheese, cranberry, corn, or milk)
3. The game board displays Wisconsin's map with different regions representing challenge categories
4. Players take turns rolling the dice, moving around the board, and answering questions
5. Correct answers earn points; incorrect answers allow other players to provide the correct answer for bonus points
6. The game ends when a player reaches the finish line
7. Scores are automatically saved to the leaderboard

#### Monitoring Student Performance

During gameplay, the system tracks each student's score and performance. After the game ends, scores are automatically saved to the leaderboard. You can view individual student performance in the **Stats** tab, which displays their total points, games played, average score, and progress over time.

### Managing Questions

#### Adding Custom Questions

The **Questions** tab displays all available questions in the question bank. Click **Add Question** to create a new question. You will see a form with the following fields:

- **Question Text:** Enter the question you want to ask students
- **Category:** Select from Healthy Behaviors, Food & Culture, Wisconsin Agriculture, Nutrition Facts, or other relevant categories
- **Correct Answer:** Enter the correct answer
- **Incorrect Answers:** Enter three plausible but incorrect answers
- **Difficulty Level:** Choose Easy, Medium, or Hard
- **Fun Fact:** Add an interesting fact related to the question (optional)

After filling in all fields, click **Save Question**. The question is immediately available for use in future games.

#### Editing and Organizing Questions

To modify a question, find it in the Questions list and click **Edit**. Make your changes and click **Save**. The updated question applies to all future games. Questions are organized by category to help students focus on specific nutrition topics. When creating or editing a question, select the appropriate category to keep your question bank organized.

### Generating Certificates

#### Creating Individual Certificates

Certificates are an excellent way to recognize student achievement. To create a certificate for a single student:

1. Click the **Certificates** tab in the Teacher Portal
2. Click **Create Individual Certificate**
3. Enter the student's name
4. Select an achievement (e.g., "Perfect Quiz Score," "Excellent Participation," "Nutrition Champion")
5. Add an optional personalized message
6. Preview the certificate to ensure accuracy
7. Click **Print** to print immediately or **Download as PDF** to save for later

#### Bulk Certificate Generation

To create certificates for an entire class at once:

1. Click the **Certificates** tab
2. Click **Batch (Entire Class)**
3. Enter student names one per line in the text area (or paste from your class roster)
4. Click **Add Names** to populate the student list
5. Review the student list and adjust as needed
6. Click **Preview All** to see thumbnail previews of all certificates
7. Click **Download All as ZIP** to download all certificates in one file
8. Extract the ZIP file and print all certificates at once

#### Customizing Certificates

You can customize certificates by adding your school logo. To add a logo:

1. In the certificate creation dialog, click **Upload School Logo**
2. Select an image file from your computer (PNG or JPG recommended)
3. The logo appears on all certificates you create
4. To remove the logo, click **Remove Logo**

### Scheduling Emails with Certificates

You can schedule emails to send certificates to colleagues, parents, or administrators. To schedule an email:

1. After creating certificates (individual or batch), click **Email**
2. Customize the email subject line and body
3. Use variables like {student_name}, {school_name}, {teacher_name}, {date} for personalization
4. Click **Preview Email** to see how the email will look
5. Select a date and time for the email to be sent
6. Click **Schedule Email**
7. The email will be sent automatically at the scheduled time

#### Managing Scheduled Emails

To view and manage your scheduled emails:

1. Click the **Scheduled** tab in the Teacher Portal
2. You will see a list of all pending scheduled emails
3. Click **Edit** to modify a scheduled email's date, time, or content
4. Click **Cancel** to delete a scheduled email
5. Once an email is sent, it moves to the "Sent" section

### Saving and Reusing Email Templates

Email templates save time when you frequently send similar messages. To save a template:

1. After customizing an email, click **Save as Template**
2. Enter a name for the template (e.g., "Standard Class Certificate Email")
3. Click **Save**

To use a saved template:

1. Open the email dialog
2. Click **Load Template**
3. Select your saved template from the list
4. The subject and body are automatically populated
5. Make any adjustments needed
6. Send or schedule as usual

To mark a template as default (so it loads automatically):

1. In the template list, click the **Star** icon next to the template name
2. The next time you open the email dialog, this template will load automatically

### Viewing and Sharing Success Stories

Success stories showcase students who have met their nutrition goals and achieved milestones. These stories provide inspiration for other students and demonstrate the impact of nutrition education.

#### Browsing Success Stories

1. Click the **Success Stories** tab in the Teacher Portal
2. You will see a gallery of success stories from your class and other classes
3. Click on a story to read the full testimonial and see engagement metrics
4. Use the search and filter options to find stories by goal type, date, or impact score

#### Creating a Success Story

To create a success story for one of your students:

1. Click **Create Success Story**
2. Enter the student's name and select their nutrition goal (e.g., "Increase vegetable consumption," "Reduce sugary drinks")
3. Write a testimonial describing how the student achieved their goal
4. Add tips that other students can use to achieve similar goals
5. Rate the student's impact (1–10 scale)
6. Click **Save**

Your success story is now visible to other teachers and can inspire their students.

#### Reacting to and Commenting on Stories

To engage with success stories:

1. Click on a story to open it
2. Click reaction buttons (👍 Like, 💡 Inspired, ✓ Helpful, 💪 Motivating) to show your appreciation
3. Scroll to the comments section and click **Add Comment** to share encouraging words
4. Your comment will be visible to other educators

### Using the AI Assistant

The **AI Assistant** is an intelligent tool that helps you create lesson plans, generate ideas for nutrition activities, and answer questions about nutrition education. The assistant is available 24/7 and learns from your interactions to provide increasingly personalized suggestions.

#### Accessing the AI Assistant

1. Click the **AI Assistant** tab in the Teacher Portal
2. You will see a chat interface with message history
3. Type your question or request in the message box
4. The AI responds with detailed, actionable suggestions

#### Example Prompts

- "Generate 5 fun nutrition lesson ideas for 3rd graders"
- "What are some creative ways to teach students about Wisconsin agriculture?"
- "Create a quiz about healthy eating habits"
- "Suggest classroom activities that promote nutrition awareness"

#### Saving and Organizing Conversations

All conversations are automatically saved and appear in the **History** sidebar. To access a previous conversation:

1. Click **History** in the AI Assistant
2. Click on any conversation to reload it
3. To delete a conversation, click the **X** icon next to it
4. To start a new conversation, click **New Conversation**

### Nutrition Roulette Game

In addition to the main board game, students can play **Nutrition Roulette**, an innovative game featuring a spinning wheel and rapid-fire challenges.

#### How Nutrition Roulette Works

1. Students access the game from the home page
2. A roulette wheel displays different challenge types
3. The wheel spins and lands on a challenge category (Trivia, Match, Recipe, Wellness, Speed)
4. Students complete the challenge within a time limit
5. Correct answers earn points and unlock power-ups
6. The leaderboard updates in real-time

#### Challenge Types

- **Trivia:** Multiple-choice questions about nutrition and Wisconsin agriculture
- **Match:** Match food items to their nutritional benefits or categories
- **Recipe:** Identify ingredients or create balanced meal combinations
- **Wellness:** Answer questions about healthy lifestyle habits
- **Speed:** Answer as many questions as possible in a limited time

### Real-Time Multiplayer Gameplay

Students can play together in real-time, with live leaderboard updates and instant feedback on correct and incorrect answers. This creates an engaging, competitive environment that motivates participation.

## Success Stories & Student Recognition

### What Are Success Stories?

Success stories are testimonials from students who have achieved nutrition goals and made positive changes in their eating habits. These stories serve multiple purposes:

- **Inspiration:** They show other students what is possible and motivate them to set their own goals
- **Recognition:** They celebrate student achievements and build confidence
- **Evidence of Impact:** They demonstrate the real-world effectiveness of nutrition education
- **Community Building:** They create a culture of health and wellness in your school

### Creating Impactful Success Stories

When writing a success story, include:

- **Student Name:** The student's first name (last name optional for privacy)
- **Goal:** The specific nutrition goal the student achieved (e.g., "Increased vegetable consumption from 1 to 3 servings per day")
- **Testimonial:** A 2–3 sentence description of how the student achieved the goal and how it made them feel
- **Tips:** 2–3 practical tips that other students can use to achieve similar goals
- **Impact Score:** A rating of 1–10 indicating how significant the achievement is

### Example Success Story

**Student Name:** Maria  
**Goal:** Reduce sugary drinks and increase water consumption  
**Testimonial:** "I used to drink soda every day, but after learning about the health effects in Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer, I decided to switch to water. Now I drink water most of the time and feel much better. My energy levels are higher, and I sleep better at night."  
**Tips:**
1. Keep a water bottle with you throughout the day
2. Flavor your water with lemon or berries if plain water seems boring
3. Track your water intake to see your progress

**Impact Score:** 8/10

## Troubleshooting & FAQs

### Common Issues and Solutions

**Q: Students cannot log in or join a class**

A: Verify that the join code is correct and has been entered exactly as displayed. Check that your network allows access to the Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer website. If the issue persists, contact your IT administrator to confirm that the platform is accessible from your school's network.

**Q: The game board is not displaying correctly**

A: Clear your browser cache and refresh the page. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari) to rule out browser-specific issues. Ensure your device has a stable internet connection. If the issue continues, contact support.

**Q: Certificates are not printing correctly**

A: Verify that your printer is connected and has paper loaded. In the print dialog, select "Print to PDF" as an alternative. Ensure your browser's print settings are configured correctly (margins, scaling, etc.). Try printing from a different device to isolate the issue.

**Q: Email templates are not saving**

A: Ensure you have entered a template name before clicking Save. The template name must be unique and cannot be empty. If you receive an error, try using a different template name or refreshing the page.

**Q: Success stories are not appearing in the gallery**

A: Verify that you have created at least one success story. Check that the story has been saved successfully (you should see a confirmation message). If stories are not visible, try refreshing the page or logging out and back in.

**Q: The AI Assistant is not responding**

A: Ensure your internet connection is stable. Try refreshing the page. If the issue persists, the AI service may be temporarily unavailable. Try again in a few minutes.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is there a cost to use Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer?**

A: No. Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer is available free to schools and education departments for testing and evaluation. There is no charge during the trial period.

**Q: How many students can join a class?**

A: There is no limit to the number of students in a class. You can have 10 students or 100 students in a single class.

**Q: Can multiple teachers share a class?**

A: Currently, each class is managed by a single teacher. If you need to co-teach a class, contact your system administrator about setting up shared access.

**Q: How long are conversations saved in the AI Assistant?**

A: Conversations are saved indefinitely in your account. You can access any previous conversation by clicking the **History** button in the AI Assistant. Conversations are private and only visible to you.

**Q: What happens to student data if a class is archived?**

A: Archived classes retain all historical data. Student scores, games played, and certificates remain in the system and are included in reports. Archived classes simply no longer appear in the active classes list.

**Q: Can I customize the certificate design?**

A: The certificate design is standardized across the platform to maintain consistency and professionalism. However, you can customize the achievement text, add a personalized message, and include your school logo.

**Q: How often are new questions added to the question bank?**

A: New questions are added regularly as teachers submit them and administrators approve them. You can request new questions on specific topics by contacting your system administrator.

**Q: Is there a way to recover deleted templates or conversations?**

A: Deleted items cannot be recovered. Before deleting a template or conversation, ensure you no longer need it. If you accidentally delete something important, contact your system administrator to see if a backup is available.

**Q: How do I report a technical issue or bug?**

A: If you encounter a technical problem, click the **Help** button (usually located in the bottom right corner of the screen) or contact your system administrator. Provide as much detail as possible about what you were doing when the issue occurred.

**Q: Can I use Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer on mobile devices?**

A: Yes. Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer is fully responsive and works on tablets and smartphones. The game board and all features are optimized for touch screens.

**Q: What is the minimum internet speed required?**

A: Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer works on standard school internet connections (5 Mbps or higher recommended). If your school has slower internet, the platform may experience occasional delays, but it should still function.

## Implementation Guide for Schools

### Planning Your Implementation

Before rolling out Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer to your school, take time to plan your implementation strategy.

#### Step 1: Assess Your Needs

- How many teachers will use the platform?
- How many students will participate?
- What are your primary goals (engagement, learning outcomes, nutrition awareness)?
- What resources do you have available (time, technology, staff training)?

#### Step 2: Identify Your Champions

- Identify 2–3 enthusiastic teachers who will pilot the platform
- These champions will become experts and help train other teachers
- Champions should represent different grade levels if possible

#### Step 3: Plan Your Rollout Timeline

- **Month 1:** Pilot with 2–3 champion teachers and their classes
- **Month 2:** Gather feedback and make adjustments
- **Month 3:** Roll out to additional teachers and classes
- **Month 4+:** Full implementation across your school or district

#### Step 4: Prepare Your Infrastructure

- Verify that all devices (computers, tablets) can access the platform
- Test the platform on your school's network to identify any issues
- Ensure you have adequate bandwidth for all students to play simultaneously
- Set up user accounts for all teachers and administrators

### Training Your Staff

Effective staff training is critical to successful implementation. Here are recommended training approaches:

#### Option 1: Group Training Session (Recommended)

- Host a 1-hour training session for all teachers
- Demonstrate the game, certificate generation, and AI Assistant
- Have teachers play a practice game
- Answer questions and address concerns
- Provide printed quick-start guides

#### Option 2: Self-Paced Online Training

- Provide links to video tutorials (see VIDEO_TUTORIALS_GUIDE.md)
- Provide access to this user manual
- Allow teachers to complete training on their own schedule
- Schedule optional follow-up sessions for questions

#### Option 3: One-on-One Coaching

- Assign a champion teacher to work with each new teacher
- Have the champion demonstrate the platform
- Have the teacher practice creating a class and playing a game
- Provide ongoing support as needed

### Measuring Success

To evaluate the effectiveness of your implementation, track these metrics:

- **Adoption:** Number of teachers using the platform and number of classes created
- **Engagement:** Number of games played per week and average student participation rate
- **Learning:** Changes in student nutrition knowledge (pre- and post-assessments)
- **Satisfaction:** Teacher and student feedback on the platform
- **Impact:** Changes in student eating habits and nutrition awareness
