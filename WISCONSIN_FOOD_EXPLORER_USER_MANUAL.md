## Introduction

Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer is an interactive educational platform designed to teach students about nutrition, Wisconsin agriculture, and healthy eating habits through engaging gameplay and practical classroom tools. This manual provides step-by-step guidance for teachers, nutrition staff, and administrators to effectively use all platform features.

The platform serves three primary user groups, each with distinct responsibilities and tools:

**Teachers** use the platform to facilitate classroom gameplay, generate certificates for student achievements, and manage class groups. **Nutrition staff** manage bulk certificate operations, customize email communications, and maintain template libraries for efficient distribution. **Administrators** oversee the entire system, including user management, question banks, and access to advanced analytics.

## Getting Started

### Accessing Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer

Open your web browser and navigate to the Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer website. You will see the home page displaying the game board with Wisconsin's map background. Three main navigation options appear in the top right corner: **Leaderboard**, **Teachers**, and your account name.

### Creating Your Account

If you do not have an account, look for a login or sign-up option on the home page. Click the login button to access the Manus authentication portal. Follow the prompts to create your account using your school email address. Once your account is created, you can log in immediately.

### Understanding Your User Role

Your user role determines which features you can access. When you first log in, the system assigns you a default role based on your account type. Teachers see the Teacher Portal, nutrition staff see bulk management tools, and administrators see the full management dashboard. If you need to change your role or access different features, contact your system administrator.

## For Teachers

### Overview of Teacher Features

The Teacher Portal provides comprehensive tools for classroom management, student engagement, and achievement recognition. Teachers can create and manage questions, organize students into classes, generate certificates, and track student performance on the leaderboard.

### Accessing the Teacher Portal

From the home page, click the **Teachers** button in the top navigation. You will be directed to the Teacher Portal dashboard. If you are not already logged in, you will be prompted to log in first. The portal displays several tabs across the top: **Questions**, **Classes**, **Stats**, **Certificates**, and **Scheduled**.

### Playing the Game with Your Class

#### Step 1: Prepare Your Classroom

Before students play the game, you need to set up your class group. In the Teacher Portal, click the **Classes** tab. You will see a list of your existing classes and an option to create a new class. Click **Create New Class** and enter your class name (for example, "3rd Grade Nutrition - Room 205"). The system generates a unique join code that students use to enter your class.

#### Step 2: Share the Join Code with Students

Display the join code on your classroom screen or write it on the board. Students can access the game from any device with internet access. On the home page, they click **Play as Student** and enter the join code to join your class. Once students enter the code, they appear in your class roster.

#### Step 3: Start a Game Session

Students can play the game individually or in groups. Each player enters their name and selects a character (cheese, cranberry, corn, or milk). The game board displays Wisconsin's map with different regions representing challenge categories. Players take turns rolling the dice, moving around the board, and answering questions about nutrition and Wisconsin agriculture. Correct answers earn points; incorrect answers allow other players to earn bonus points by providing the correct answer.

#### Step 4: Monitor Student Performance

During gameplay, the system tracks each student's score and performance. After the game ends, scores are automatically saved to the leaderboard. You can view individual student performance in the **Stats** tab, which displays their total points, games played, and average score.

### Managing Questions

#### Adding Custom Questions

The **Questions** tab displays all available questions in the question bank. Click **Add Question** to create a new question. You will see a form with the following fields:

- **Question Text:** Enter the question you want to ask students
- **Category:** Select from Healthy Behaviors, Food & Culture, Question, or another category
- **Correct Answer:** Enter the correct answer
- **Incorrect Answers:** Enter three plausible but incorrect answers
- **Difficulty Level:** Choose Easy, Medium, or Hard
- **Fun Fact:** Add an interesting fact related to the question (optional)

After filling in all fields, click **Save Question**. The question is immediately available for use in future games.

#### Editing Existing Questions

To modify a question, find it in the Questions list and click **Edit**. Make your changes and click **Save**. The updated question applies to all future games.

#### Organizing Questions by Category

Questions are organized by category to help students focus on specific nutrition topics. When creating or editing a question, select the appropriate category. Common categories include Healthy Behaviors, Food & Culture, Wisconsin Agriculture, and Nutrition Facts.

### Generating Certificates

#### Creating Individual Certificates

The **Certificates** tab provides tools for recognizing student achievements. To create a certificate for a single student, click **Individual Certificate**. A form appears with the following fields:

- **Student Name:** Enter the student's full name
- **Achievement:** Select from predefined achievements (for example, "Perfect Score," "Quiz Master," "Nutrition Expert") or enter a custom achievement
- **Date:** The current date is pre-filled; change it if needed
- **Message:** Add a personalized message (optional)

After completing the form, click **Preview** to see how the certificate will look. The preview displays the Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer logo, the student's name, the achievement, and your school logo if you have uploaded one. Click **Print** to print the certificate or **Download as PDF** to save it to your computer.

#### Generating Bulk Certificates for Your Class

To create certificates for multiple students at once, click **Batch (Entire Class)**. A text area appears where you can enter student names, one per line. For example:

```
Alice Johnson
Bob Smith
Charlie Davis
Diana Martinez
```

Click **Add Names** to populate the list. The system displays all students with checkboxes. Select the students you want to include in the batch. Click **Preview All** to see thumbnails of all certificates. Once you are satisfied, click **Download All as ZIP** to download a single file containing all certificates as images.

#### Emailing Certificates Directly

Instead of downloading certificates, you can email them directly to your registered email address. After adding student names and selecting the students, click **Email ZIP (4)** (the number indicates how many certificates are included). A dialog appears with the following options:

- **Conversation Title:** Enter a title for your reference (for example, "3rd Grade Nutrition Certificates - January 2026")
- **Email Subject:** Customize the subject line (for example, "Your Class Certificates - Nutrition Achievement")
- **Email Body:** Write a personalized message to yourself or your colleagues

The dialog displays available variables you can use in your email: **{teacher_name}**, **{school_name}**, **{student_count}**, and **{date}**. These variables automatically replace with actual values when the email is sent. For example, if you write "Congratulations to all {student_count} students in {school_name}," the email will read "Congratulations to all 4 students in Lincoln Elementary School."

Click **Preview Email** to see exactly how the email will appear with all variables substituted. Click **Send ZIP to Email** to send the certificates as a ZIP attachment.

#### Saving Email Templates for Future Use

If you frequently send certificates with similar messages, save your email template for quick reuse. After customizing the subject and body, enter a template name (for example, "Standard Class Certificate Email") and click **Save**. The template is saved to your account.

To use a saved template in the future, click **Email ZIP** and look for the **Email Templates** section at the top of the dialog. Your saved templates appear in a dropdown list. Click **Load** next to a template to instantly populate the subject and body fields with your saved content.

#### Managing Your Email Templates

In the **Email Templates** section, you can view all your saved templates. Each template shows its name and subject line preview. Click the **Load** button to use a template, click the **Star** button to mark it as your default template (the star fills in when selected), or click the **Delete** button to remove a template you no longer need.

### Using the AI Assistant for Lesson Planning

The **AI Assistant** button appears in the top right corner of the Teacher Portal. Click it to open a chatbot that provides educational support, lesson ideas, resources, and teaching strategies.

#### Accessing Different Chatbot Modes

The chatbot offers five specialized modes, each designed for different types of support:

- **General Support:** Ask general questions about teaching, classroom management, or any topic
- **Lesson Ideas:** Request creative lesson plans, activities, and classroom strategies
- **Resources:** Find links to educational materials, worksheets, and teaching tools
- **Fun Trivia:** Learn interesting facts about food history, Wisconsin agriculture, and nutrition
- **Challenges:** Get ideas for classroom challenges, competitions, and student engagement activities

Click on any mode button to switch modes. The chatbot's responses adjust based on the selected mode.

#### Starting a Conversation

Type your question or request in the text box at the bottom and click the send button. For example, in **Lesson Ideas** mode, you might ask: "Give me 3 fun and interactive lesson ideas for teaching elementary students about healthy eating habits and nutrition." The AI responds with detailed, actionable suggestions.

#### Saving Conversations for Later

All conversations are automatically saved. To view your previous conversations, click the **History** button. A list of your saved conversations appears, showing the conversation title, date, and number of messages. Click on any conversation to resume it from where you left off.

To start a fresh conversation, click the **New** button. Enter an optional title (for example, "Nutrition Lesson Planning") and begin typing your question.

#### Clearing a Conversation

To delete all messages in the current conversation and start fresh, click the **Clear Chat** button. This removes all messages from the current session but does not delete saved conversations in your history.

## For Nutrition Staff

### Overview of Nutrition Staff Features

Nutrition staff use Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer to manage bulk certificate distribution, customize email communications with teachers and students, maintain reusable email templates, and track certificate distribution history. These tools streamline the process of recognizing student achievements across multiple classrooms and schools.

### Accessing Bulk Certificate Management

From the Teacher Portal, navigate to the **Certificates** tab and select **Batch (Entire Class)**. This section allows you to manage certificates for multiple students at once.

### Managing Bulk Certificate Operations

#### Adding Students in Bulk

The batch certificate interface provides two methods for adding student names. The first method is manual entry: click in the text area and type student names, one per line. The second method is CSV upload: click **Upload CSV** and select a file from your computer. The CSV file should have one student name per line with no header row.

After entering or uploading names, click **Add Names**. The system displays all students with checkboxes. Review the list to ensure all names are correct. If you need to remove a student, uncheck their checkbox.

#### Previewing Certificates Before Distribution

Before sending or downloading certificates, always preview them to ensure accuracy. Click **Preview All** to see thumbnail images of all certificates. Scroll through the previews to check that names are spelled correctly and the formatting looks professional. If you notice an error, go back and correct the student name in the list.

#### Downloading Certificates as ZIP

Once you have previewed the certificates and confirmed they are correct, click **Download All as ZIP**. Your browser downloads a single ZIP file containing all certificates as image files. The filename includes the date and number of certificates (for example, "certificates_2026-01-23_4.zip"). Extract the ZIP file on your computer to access individual certificate images.

### Customizing and Sending Emails

#### Preparing to Send Certificates via Email

Instead of downloading certificates, you can email them directly. Click **Email ZIP (4)** (the number indicates the count of certificates). A dialog appears with email customization options.

#### Customizing Email Content

The email customization dialog includes three main sections:

**Email Subject:** This field contains a default subject line. You can modify it to match your needs. For example, change "Your Certificate ZIP File" to "Achievement Certificates - January 2026 Nutrition Challenge."

**Email Body:** This larger text area contains the email message. You can write a personalized message to accompany the certificates. The body supports variables that automatically substitute with actual values: **{teacher_name}**, **{school_name}**, **{student_count}**, and **{date}**.

**Character Counter:** Below the email body, a counter displays the current character count and the maximum allowed (5000 characters). This helps you keep emails concise and readable.

#### Previewing Your Email

Before sending, always preview the email to see exactly how it will appear. Click **Preview Email** to open a preview panel. The preview shows the subject line and body text with all variables replaced by actual values. For example, if your template includes "{student_count} students from {school_name}," the preview displays "4 students from Lincoln Elementary School."

#### Sending the Email

After customizing and previewing the email, click **Send ZIP to Email**. The system sends the email to your registered email address with the ZIP file attached. You receive a confirmation message indicating the email was sent successfully.

### Creating and Managing Email Templates

#### Saving a New Template

After customizing an email subject and body, you can save it as a template for future use. In the **Email Templates** section at the top of the email dialog, enter a template name (for example, "Standard Nutrition Achievement Email") and click **Save**. The template is immediately saved to your account.

#### Loading a Saved Template

To use a saved template, click **Email ZIP** to open the email customization dialog. In the **Email Templates** section, your saved templates appear in a list. Each template shows its name and a preview of the subject line. Click **Load** next to the template you want to use. The subject and body fields automatically populate with your saved content.

#### Marking a Template as Default

If you have a template you use frequently, mark it as your default. In the **Email Templates** list, click the **Star** icon next to the template. The star fills in to indicate this is your default template. The next time you open the email customization dialog, this template loads automatically.

#### Deleting a Template

To remove a template you no longer need, click the **Delete** button next to it in the **Email Templates** list. A confirmation dialog appears asking you to confirm the deletion. Click **Confirm** to permanently delete the template.

### Tracking Email Distribution History

The system maintains a record of all emails you send. This history helps you track which certificates have been distributed and when. To view your email distribution history, look for a **Distribution History** or **Sent Emails** section in the Certificates tab (if available in your version). The history displays the date sent, recipient, number of certificates, and email subject line.

## For Administrators

### Overview of Administrator Features

Administrators have access to comprehensive management tools for the entire Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer platform. These tools include user management, question bank administration, class oversight, analytics and reporting, and system configuration.

### Accessing the Administrator Dashboard

From the home page, click your account name in the top right corner and select **Administrator Dashboard** (this option only appears for users with administrator privileges). The dashboard displays an overview of system statistics, including total users, active classes, certificates issued, and platform usage metrics.

### Managing Users and Roles

#### Viewing All Users

In the Administrator Dashboard, click the **Users** section. A table displays all registered users, including their name, email, school, user role (teacher, nutrition staff, administrator), and account creation date. Use the search box to find a specific user by name or email.

#### Assigning or Changing User Roles

To change a user's role, click on their name in the user table. A detail panel opens showing their account information. Click **Edit Role** and select the new role from the dropdown (Teacher, Nutrition Staff, or Administrator). Click **Save** to apply the change. The user's access permissions update immediately.

#### Deactivating User Accounts

If a user should no longer have access to the platform (for example, if they have left your school), click their name and select **Deactivate Account**. A confirmation dialog appears. Click **Confirm** to deactivate the account. Deactivated accounts cannot log in, but their historical data (scores, certificates, etc.) remains in the system for record-keeping.

### Managing the Question Bank

#### Reviewing All Questions

Click the **Questions** section in the Administrator Dashboard. A table displays all questions in the system, including the question text, category, difficulty level, and the teacher who created it. Use filters to view questions by category or difficulty level.

#### Approving New Questions

Teachers can submit new questions for inclusion in the main question bank. Submitted questions appear in a separate **Pending Approval** section. Review each question to ensure it is accurate, age-appropriate, and relevant to the curriculum. Click **Approve** to add the question to the active question bank or **Reject** to decline it. If you reject a question, optionally provide feedback to the teacher explaining why.

#### Editing or Removing Questions

If you find an error in an existing question or need to update it, click the question in the table and select **Edit**. Make your changes and click **Save**. If a question is inappropriate or no longer relevant, click **Remove** to delete it from the question bank.

#### Organizing Questions by Category

Questions are organized into categories to help students focus on specific topics. Common categories include Healthy Behaviors, Food & Culture, Wisconsin Agriculture, and Nutrition Facts. When reviewing questions, ensure they are assigned to the correct category. If you need to create a new category, click **Add Category** and enter the category name.

### Viewing Analytics and Reports

#### Accessing the Analytics Dashboard

Click the **Analytics** section in the Administrator Dashboard. This section displays comprehensive data about platform usage, student engagement, and achievement metrics.

#### Understanding Key Metrics

The analytics dashboard displays several important metrics:

**Total Games Played:** The cumulative number of games played across all classes and students. This metric helps you understand overall platform engagement.

**Average Score:** The average score across all games played. This metric indicates the overall difficulty level of questions and student comprehension.

**Most Popular Questions:** A list of the questions answered most frequently. These questions are engaging and relevant to students.

**Certificates Issued:** The total number of certificates generated through the platform. This metric tracks achievement recognition usage.

**Active Classes:** The number of classes currently active on the platform. This metric helps you understand adoption across your school or district.

#### Generating Custom Reports

To generate a report for a specific time period or user group, click **Generate Report**. A form appears with options to select the date range, user group (all users, specific school, specific class), and metrics to include (games played, scores, certificates, etc.). Click **Generate** to create the report. The system displays the report on screen and provides an option to download it as a PDF or CSV file.

### Managing Classes and Groups

#### Viewing All Classes

Click the **Classes** section to see a table of all classes on the platform. Each row displays the class name, teacher name, number of students, and creation date. Use the search box to find a specific class.

#### Monitoring Class Activity

Click on a class name to view detailed information about that class. The detail panel shows the class roster (all students enrolled), recent game sessions, and achievement statistics. You can see which students have played games, their scores, and certificates they have earned.

#### Archiving Inactive Classes

If a class is no longer active (for example, at the end of the school year), you can archive it. Click the class name and select **Archive**. Archived classes no longer appear in the active classes list, but their historical data remains accessible for reporting purposes.

### Configuring System Settings

#### Accessing System Settings

Click the **Settings** section in the Administrator Dashboard. This section contains configuration options for the entire platform.

#### Managing Email and Communication Settings

In the Settings section, you can configure email settings for the platform. Enter your SMTP server details (if using a custom email provider) or use the default email service. You can also set the "from" email address that appears on all system-generated emails.

#### Configuring School Information

Enter your school or district name, logo URL, and primary contact information. This information appears on certificates, emails, and public-facing pages. If you have a school logo, upload it here. The logo automatically appears on all generated certificates.

#### Setting Up Custom Branding

You can customize the platform's appearance to match your school's branding. Upload your school logo, select your school's primary and secondary colors, and choose a custom domain name (if available). These customizations appear throughout the platform, creating a cohesive branded experience.

### Using the AI Assistant for Administrative Insights

The AI Assistant is available to administrators as well. Click the **AI Assistant** button to access the chatbot. In **General Support** mode, you can ask questions about platform administration, best practices for implementing the platform in your school, or strategies for increasing student engagement. The AI provides detailed, actionable guidance based on educational research and best practices.

## Troubleshooting & FAQs

### Common Issues and Solutions

#### Issue: I Cannot Log In to My Account

**Solution:** Verify that you are using the correct email address associated with your account. If you have forgotten your password, click **Forgot Password** on the login page. You will receive an email with instructions to reset your password. Check your spam folder if you do not see the email within a few minutes.

If you still cannot log in after resetting your password, contact your system administrator. They can verify that your account is active and has the correct permissions.

#### Issue: Students Cannot Join My Class

**Solution:** Verify that you have created a class and generated a join code. The join code is a unique identifier that students use to enter your class. Make sure you are sharing the correct code with students. Join codes are case-insensitive, so students can enter them in uppercase or lowercase.

If students are entering the correct code but still cannot join, check that your class is active (not archived). If the issue persists, contact your system administrator.

#### Issue: Certificates Are Not Printing Correctly

**Solution:** Ensure your printer is connected and has paper loaded. When printing certificates, use the print preview to verify the layout before printing. If the certificate appears cut off or misaligned in the preview, adjust your printer settings. Most printers have options for margins and scaling. Set margins to 0.5 inches and scaling to 100%.

If you are printing to PDF instead of a physical printer, ensure you have selected the correct PDF printer in your print dialog.

#### Issue: Email With Certificates Is Not Arriving

**Solution:** Check your spam or junk email folder. System-generated emails sometimes get filtered as spam. If you find the email in your spam folder, mark it as "not spam" so future emails arrive in your inbox.

If the email is not in your spam folder, verify that you entered your email address correctly when sending. Check the email address in your account profile to ensure it is correct.

#### Issue: The AI Assistant Is Not Responding

**Solution:** Ensure you have a stable internet connection. The AI Assistant requires an active connection to function. If your connection is slow, responses may take longer than usual.

If the AI Assistant is still not responding after checking your connection, try refreshing the page. If the issue persists, contact your system administrator.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer on mobile devices?**

A: Yes. Wisconsin Nutrition Explorer is designed to work on smartphones, tablets, and computers. The interface automatically adjusts to fit your device's screen size. For the best experience, use a device with a screen at least 5 inches wide.

**Q: How many students can be in a class?**

A: There is no limit to the number of students in a class. However, for optimal gameplay experience, we recommend keeping classes to 30 students or fewer during simultaneous gameplay.

**Q: Can I export student scores and performance data?**

A: Yes. Administrators can generate reports that include student scores, games played, and other performance metrics. Reports can be downloaded as PDF or CSV files. Teachers can view their own class's performance data in the Stats tab.

**Q: How often are new questions added to the question bank?**

A: New questions are added regularly as teachers submit them and administrators approve them. You can request new questions on specific topics by contacting your system administrator.

**Q: Can I customize the certificate design?**

A: The certificate design is standardized across the platform to maintain consistency and professionalism. However, you can customize the achievement text, add a personalized message, and include your school logo.

**Q: How long are conversations saved in the AI Assistant?**

A: Conversations are saved indefinitely in your account. You can access any previous conversation by clicking the **History** button in the AI Assistant. Conversations are private and only visible to you.

**Q: What happens to student data if a class is archived?**

A: Archived classes retain all historical data. Student scores, games played, and certificates remain in the system and are included in reports. Archived classes simply no longer appear in the active classes list.

**Q: Can multiple teachers share a class?**

A: Currently, each class is managed by a single teacher. If you need to co-teach a class, contact your system administrator about setting up shared access.

**Q: Is there a way to recover deleted templates or conversations?**

A: Deleted items cannot be recovered. Before deleting a template or conversation, ensure you no longer need it. If you accidentally delete something important, contact your system administrator to see if a backup is available.

**Q: How do I report a technical issue or bug?**

A: If you encounter a technical problem, click the **Help** button (usually located in the bottom right corner of the screen) or contact your system administrator. Provide as much detail as possible about what you were doing when the issue occurred.
