# Wisconsin Food Explorer - Feedback Collection Guide

**Gathering User Insights During Trial Period**

---

## Overview

Feedback collection is critical during the trial period. User insights help us understand what's working, what needs improvement, and how to better serve schools. This guide provides frameworks and templates for collecting actionable feedback.

---

## Feedback Collection Methods

### 1. In-App Feedback Form (Immediate)

**When:** After users complete key actions (first game, first certificate, first week)  
**Format:** Simple pop-up or modal dialog  
**Time:** 30 seconds to 2 minutes

**Questions:**

1. **How easy was this to use?** (1–5 scale with emoji)
   - 😞 Very difficult
   - 😕 Somewhat difficult
   - 😐 Neutral
   - 🙂 Somewhat easy
   - 😄 Very easy

2. **What was your favorite part?** (Open text, optional)

3. **What could we improve?** (Open text, optional)

**Implementation:** Show after first game completion, first certificate creation, and after one week of usage.

---

### 2. Weekly Email Survey (Structured)

**When:** Every Friday for the first 4 weeks  
**Format:** Email with embedded survey link  
**Time:** 3–5 minutes

**Survey Questions:**

**Section 1: Overall Experience**
- How satisfied are you with Wisconsin Food Explorer? (1–5 scale)
- How likely are you to recommend this platform to other teachers? (0–10 scale)

**Section 2: Feature Feedback**
- Which features have you used this week?
  - [ ] Game board
  - [ ] Certificates
  - [ ] AI Assistant
  - [ ] Success Stories
  - [ ] Analytics/Stats
  - [ ] Other: ___

- Which feature was most valuable? (Dropdown)
- Which feature needs improvement? (Dropdown)

**Section 3: Challenges**
- Have you encountered any technical issues? (Yes/No)
  - If yes, please describe: ___

- What would make the platform easier to use?

**Section 4: Student Feedback**
- How are your students responding to the game?
  - [ ] Very engaged
  - [ ] Somewhat engaged
  - [ ] Neutral
  - [ ] Not very engaged
  - [ ] Not engaged at all

- What do students like most about the game?

**Section 5: Open Comments**
- Any other feedback or suggestions?

---

### 3. Post-Trial Interview (In-Depth)

**When:** At end of trial period (Week 4–6)  
**Format:** 15–20 minute phone or video call  
**Participants:** School administrator and 1–2 champion teachers

**Interview Guide:**

**Opening (2 minutes)**
- Thank you for participating in the trial
- Purpose: Understand your experience and gather feedback
- This will help us improve the platform

**Overall Experience (3 minutes)**
- Tell me about your experience with Wisconsin Food Explorer
- What went well?
- What was challenging?

**Feature Feedback (5 minutes)**
- Which features did you use most?
- Which features were most valuable?
- Which features need improvement?
- What features are missing?

**Student Engagement (3 minutes)**
- How did students respond to the game?
- Did you notice any changes in student motivation or participation?
- Any memorable student feedback?

**Implementation (3 minutes)**
- How smooth was the implementation process?
- What support did you need?
- What would have made implementation easier?

**Next Steps (2 minutes)**
- Would you be interested in continuing to use the platform?
- Would you recommend it to other schools?
- What would it take for you to expand to more classes/teachers?

**Closing (2 minutes)**
- Thank you for your time and feedback
- Next steps and timeline
- Contact information for follow-up questions

---

### 4. Focus Group Discussion (Collaborative)

**When:** Mid-trial (Week 2–3)  
**Format:** 1-hour group discussion  
**Participants:** 4–6 teachers from different grade levels

**Agenda:**

**Welcome & Icebreaker (5 minutes)**
- Introductions and thank you for participation
- Overview of discussion format

**Initial Impressions (10 minutes)**
- What was your first impression of Wisconsin Food Explorer?
- Was setup and getting started easy?
- Any surprises (good or bad)?

**Feature Exploration (20 minutes)**
- Game board: What works? What could be better?
- Certificates: How are you using them? Any issues?
- AI Assistant: Have you tried it? What was your experience?
- Success Stories: Are you reading/creating them?
- Analytics: Are you using the stats? Useful?

**Student Feedback (10 minutes)**
- How are your students responding?
- Which aspects do students enjoy most?
- Any student feedback to share?
- Engagement levels compared to other activities?

**Challenges and Support (10 minutes)**
- What challenges have you encountered?
- What support would be helpful?
- Any technical issues?
- Suggestions for improvement?

**Closing (5 minutes)**
- Summary of key themes
- Next steps
- Thank you and appreciation

### Net Promoter Score (NPS) Survey

**Question:** How likely are you to recommend Wisconsin Food Explorer to a colleague?

**Scale:** 0–10 (0 = Not at all likely, 10 = Extremely likely)

**Follow-up:** Why did you give that score?

**Scoring:**
- 9–10 = Promoter (enthusiastic advocate)
- 7–8 = Passive (satisfied but not enthusiastic)
- 0–6 = Detractor (dissatisfied, may discourage others)

**Target:** Aim for 50%+ Promoters by end of trial

## Feedback Analysis Framework

### Categorizing Feedback

**Positive Feedback:**
- What's working well?
- What features are most valued?
- What surprised users positively?

**Constructive Feedback:**
- What needs improvement?
- What's frustrating or confusing?
- What's missing?

**Actionable Feedback:**
- What specific changes would make the biggest impact?
- What's preventing adoption?
- What would increase usage?

### Prioritization Matrix

Use this matrix to prioritize feedback:

| Impact | Effort | Priority | Examples |
|--------|--------|----------|----------|
| High | Low | **Do First** | Bug fixes, quick feature additions |
| High | High | **Plan** | Major features, significant improvements |
| Low | Low | **Nice to Have** | Polish, minor enhancements |
| Low | High | **Deprioritize** | Complex features with low demand |

## Key Metrics to Track

### Adoption Metrics
- % of teachers using platform
- % of students playing at least once
- Average games played per student
- Certificate generation rate

### Engagement Metrics
- Daily active users
- Average session duration
- Feature usage rates
- Return user rate

### Satisfaction Metrics
- NPS (Net Promoter Score)
- Feature satisfaction scores
- Overall satisfaction rating
- Recommendation likelihood

### Feedback Metrics
- Feedback response rate
- Feedback sentiment (positive/neutral/negative)
- Top feature requests
- Top reported issues

## Privacy and Data Protection

### Feedback Data Handling
- All feedback is confidential
- User names are optional
- Feedback is used only for product improvement
- No feedback is shared with third parties
- Users can request deletion of their feedback

### Compliance
- FERPA compliant (no student data in feedback)
- GDPR compliant (for international users)
- Regular security audits
- Encrypted data storage
